---
title: On skepticism in therapy
date: 2026-03-10
---

Men who are skeptical about therapy are often the easiest to work with.

The skepticism usually means they're paying attention — that they have some standard they're holding therapy to. If there's a standard, there's something they'd actually want from it. That's something to work with.

What's harder is indifference: showing up, going through the motions, waiting for it to be over. Skepticism implies a question. Indifference implies there's no question left to ask.

If you're skeptical and reading this, that's not a problem. It's actually a reasonable place to start.
