---
title: On naming patterns
date: 2026-01-15
---

A lot of what happens in therapy is naming. Not diagnosing — naming. Giving something a label that fits, that the person actually recognizes.

The pattern was already there before the word. But the word changes what you can do with it. It makes the pattern visible from the outside, even while you're still inside it.

Suppression and avoidance are useful patterns. They work in the short term. That's exactly why they persist. The goal isn't to eliminate them — it's to have enough language around them that you can see when they're running the show.

That visibility is usually where the work starts.
